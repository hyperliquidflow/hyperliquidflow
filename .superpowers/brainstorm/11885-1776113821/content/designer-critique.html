UI Designer's Honest Critique

What was wrong with Options A-E, and why. Then: the redesign.

What broke in the previous 5 layouts
No hierarchy
Every card, chart, and table gets the same visual weight. Stat cards (secondary data) sit at the same prominence as the signal feed (primary data). The user's eye has nowhere to go first.
Redundant data channels
The ticker strip, smart money exposure bars, and stat cards all communicate coin prominence. Three different widgets saying the same thing — different format, same signal.
Option C bubbles are misleading
Y-axis has no meaning — bubbles are randomly positioned. EV score differences of 0.01–0.09 are imperceptible as bubble radii. Looks visual, communicates nothing. Financial charts need legible axes.
Option E rings are dead
Wallet scores range 0.00–0.07. Rings are nearly all empty. The visualization reveals nothing — it's visually interesting but informationally bankrupt for this specific data.
Stats as big cards = wrong priority
"200 wallets tracked" and "Avg Score 0.02" are context, not action items. Giving them full-height frosted cards is like bolding every word in a sentence — the emphasis is everywhere and therefore nowhere.
Vibes + Exposure always orphaned
Market Vibes (7-day dots) and Smart Money Exposure are tertiary. Every layout puts them in a bottom row with equal weight to signals and wallets. They should be compressed, not featured.
What was right: the heatmap
Coin × hour matrix in Option A is the strongest idea. It's genuinely novel, scannable, and reveals patterns invisible in the bar chart. Needs more space and cleaner execution.
What was right: regime timeline bar
Option D's 7-day regime as a full-width colored bar row is excellent — compact, beautiful, informative. Replace the dots widget entirely with this.
